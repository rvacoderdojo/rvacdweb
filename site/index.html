About RVA Coder Dojo

RVA Coder Dojo is a free, volunteer led, open source resource to help kids, ages 5-17, understand how to become digital producers.

Most children are outstanding digital consumers. Very few understand how the technology they consume is created. We seek to introduce and inspire kids to learn and explore programming, digital and technology design.

Computer science develops studentsâ€™ computational and critical thinking skills and shows them how to create, not simply use, new technologies. This fundamental knowledge is needed to prepare students for the 21st century, regardless of their ultimate field of study or occupation.

We embrace the philosophy of "failing forward". We believe that through both success and failure, we create the foundations for knowledge.

HTML Remixed

Experience Level: Beginner

Recommended Ages: 9-14

Working with a starter web page, the children will be guided through "remixing" the web page to create their own unique page.

Along the way they will be exposed to HTML, the basic language for building web pages, and CSS the core language for crafting the appearance of web pages.

Computer Science Unplugged

Experience Level: Beginner

Recommended Ages: 5-13

No computers, just fun and learning! What is a "logical condition"? What's an "Algorithm"? How do programmers "decompose" a problem?

In this session children will have the opportunity to learn these important coding concepts and more using real world objects and interactive "play" offline. We will use common every day objects like paper, pencils, and playing cards to help guide the students to thinking like a coder while letting them use their own creative ideas.

Event Information and Registration

Scratch for Beginners

Experience Level: Beginner

Recommended Ages: 7-12

This session will be geared towards learning the basics of Scratch programming. Scratch is a visually oriented language for kids to learn programming concepts. During this session we will introduce Scratch programming through a series of very basic challenges.

This class is recommended for children who have never used Scratch before, or those who want a more basic introduction to using Scratch.

Whether your child has never seen a line of code, or already has their own app in the App Store, there will be an activity here for them.
